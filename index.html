A Short History of Live Music

  • 1672: The first known public concerts for which admission was charged are given in London by the violinist John Banister.
  • 1678: Thomas Britton, a charcoal seller, establishes weekly concerts in a loft in Clerkenwell at the subscription rate of 10 shillings a year.
  • 17th century: Opera becomes a popular form of entertainment in Europe, and opera houses are built throughout the continent.
  • 18th century: Orchestras begin to grow and become more prominent, and concerts featuring orchestral music become popular.
  • 19th century: New technologies, such as the phonograph and the gramophone, make it possible for people to listen to music at home, but live music remains a popular form of entertainment.
  • 20th century: New genres of music, such as jazz, blues, and rock and roll, emerge, and these genres are often associated with live performance. Rock festivals become popular in the 1960s.
  • 21st century: Live music remains a popular form of entertainment, and people attend concerts to see their favorite musicians perform, to experience the energy of a live performance, and to be part of a community of fans.

    Fun Facts About Dance

  • Cave paintings depicting dancing figures are found in India, suggesting that dance has been a part of human culture for at least 9000 years.
  • Dance becomes an important part of religious and spiritual ceremonies in ancient Egypt, Greece, and India, and is often used to express devotion to the gods.
  • The first ballet schools are established in Italy in the 15th century, and ballet begins to develop into a sophisticated art form.
  • The Renaissance sees a revival of interest in dance, and new dances are invented, such as the minuet and the gavotte.
  • Dance is used to express emotions and tell stories in the 17th and 18th centuries, with the development of new genres such as opera ballet and pantomime.
  • Ballet becomes even more popular in the 19th century, with the work of choreographers such as Marius Petipa and Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky.
  • Modern dance emerges in the late 19th century and early 20th century as a reaction against the strict rules and conventions of classical ballet.
  • Dance becomes more accessible to the public in the 20th century, with the development of new dance forms such as jazz dance, hip hop dance, and breakdancing.
  • Dance continues to evolve and diversify in the 21st century, with new dance styles emerging all the time.
